More Information about Chinese Discrimination

     Chinese discrimination has been around for years, but it has been most common when the COVID-19 pandemic.
This virus originated from China, and people of other races blame the Chinese for starting this pandemic. This
caused other races to perform violent actions and crimes against them. Three in ten Chinese people experienced
discrimination in the five years preceding 2019, or the years after the pandemic started.

     Chinese people mostly experience discrimination in a store, bank, or restaurant. When it came to discrimination,
Chinese people most often experience discrimation on race, ethnicity, and language. Other reasons for the
discrimination against them are physical appearance, sex, age, and gender identity.

About one-third of Asians know another Asian who has been attacked or threatened after the pandemic. Chinese
women are also most likely to be threatened or attacked. Chinese people between the ages of 18-29 are also most
likely to be threatened or attacked.
